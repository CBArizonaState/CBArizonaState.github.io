Brain–Computer Interfaces

Brain–Computer Interfaces (BCIs) are systems that create a direct communication pathway between the brain and external devices, translating neural activity into digital commands. They enable control of computers, prosthetics, or communication tools by decoding electrical signals generated by neurons.
